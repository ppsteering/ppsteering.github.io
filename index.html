Proxy Policy Steering

Anonymous Authors
Abstract

Robotics foundation models carry broad manipulation priors from large-scale data, but specializing them to a new task remains the deployment bottleneck. This requires injecting new task-specific behavior from limited demonstrations without eroding their broad capabilities. We introduce Proxy Policy Steering (PPS), a novel adaptation method that addresses this challenge by training two lightweight proxy policies. A reference proxy models the frozen base's behavior on target-task observations, and a task proxy, initialized from the reference, captures how this behavior changes under task supervision. Their difference forms a calibrated velocity-space residual that steers the frozen base sampler at every denoising step. We identify the conditions under which this residual isolates the change induced by task supervision, and validate them empirically. On 8 real-world and 4 simulation manipulation tasks, PPS lifts the state-of-the-art π0.5 base policy by 53% absolute success rate on average, with 0-to-1 gains on tasks the base never solves, while preserving the base's broad capabilities. PPS outperforms LoRA fine-tuning, from-scratch specialists, residual policies, and prior inference-time steering methods.

Method

PPS trains two small proxy policies and uses their velocity difference to steer the frozen base sampler:

  • Reference proxy vref: distilled from the base's denoising trajectories on the target task. It captures the base-like behavior on this specific task that the proxy architecture can express.
  • Task proxy vtask: initialized from vref, then fine-tuned on task demonstrations with the standard flow-matching loss.

At inference, PPS integrates the guided velocity field:

vPPS(x, k, o, l) = vbase(x, k, o, l) + γ · [ vtask(x, k, o) − vref(x, k, o) ]

their velocity-space residual captures the task-induced distribution shift and steers the frozen base policy at inference to apprximate a fine-tuned distribution.

+53% on Real-world Tasks

We evaluate PPS on 8 real-world manipulation tasks, spanning long-horizon goals, articulated and deformable objects, and diverse skills. The benchmark mixes tasks the base model has seen during pretraining with challenging out-of-distribution tasks. PPS delivers consistent gains over the base model and outperforms fine-tuned models, training-from-scratch policies, and prior steering-based baselines.

+51% on Simulation Tasks

In simulation, PPS lifts the π0.5 base policy by 51% absolute success rate on average across four tasks, outperforming LoRA, specialist, residual, and DSRL baselines on every task.

Robustness under Perturbations

Because PPS leaves the base model intact, the combined policy inherits the base's board priors and shows robust recovery behaviors even no recovery data is collected in the demonstrations. Whereas LoRA fine-tuning overfits to the limited demonstrations and loses these priors, leading to failures under perturbations.

Cross-Modality Steering

The proxy architecture is independent of the base model's input design. Therefore, PPS can route extra modalities like point cloud or audio into the base model through the task proxy. Here, the poin cloud input helps to locate the texture-less object, and the audio input distinguishes the rining phone from the silent one. This makes PPS a flexible enhancement module for possible hardware updates.
